About

I am a physicist trying to understand the complexities of our universe. In today's era of abundant data, I am interested in using this information to comprehend how various interconnected systems function together. My focus lies in exploring how collective phenomena emerge, adapt, evolve, and endure through changes. I study Complex Systems, primarily developing models for epidemic spreading, but I have also worked on non-linear random walks. Currently, I am working at Tecnológico de Monterrey as a lecturer for engineers. I teach intensive introductory physics courses, covering topics such as mechanics, thermodynamics, electrostatics, electromagnetism, and statistics.
